Sassy Cat Studios Branding

A exploration into a freelance/studio branding

Date: On Going

Title: Sassy Cat Studios Branding

Job Titles: Illustrator, Copywriter, Graphic Designer

Software/hardware: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop

Summary

This is the branding that I developed initially in high school, and then through level one and two of the IMD program. If I were to open my own studio this is the branding I would want it under. The faceless cat is something that has stayed with me for nearly 20 years, so if it has lasted that long for me I think that says something. I would like to blend the old with the new.

The Original

This is the original design that I came up with used on a stationary set. The font choice was a very uneducated choice and they don’t match. I do still love the way I used the cat on the letterhead. Outside of that I hadn’t applied the logo in any other way.

The Updated

I turned my original logo into a brand through a few of my classes in my IMD program. In Digital Graphics, I redesigned my logo and came up with a mini branding guide. In User Experience I, I continued to brand myself through personas, mood boards, and wireframes. In Web Dev II we applied that branding to a web page that was developed as our final. For now I am going to develop and refine this branding until I am going to do more freelance work.

My favorite part about this branding is the icon that I made using the initials and the three stars of the logo. I also utilized that icon into a repeating background motif. I also like how there is the space where the word "Studios" fits and if the studio gets large enough for multiple departments, the name of the department can fit there. That is featured on page two of the branding guide.
