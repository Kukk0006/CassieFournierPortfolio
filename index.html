About Me

I'm a student at Algonquin College of Applied Arts and Technology, on track to graduate in April 2024. My college ride has been quite the adventure, exploring a mix of subjects—from web design and photography to videography and UX design. But you know what really caught my eye? Motion graphics! During a group project in the video production course, I took charge of creating most of the motion graphics used. That experience got me hyped up for an upcoming dedicated course in that field. Exploring motion graphics got me hooked because it's just so fascinating and has a combination of creativity and logic!

I'm all about soaking up new knowledge—I dive into tutorials across a wide range of topics. My only setback is sometimes not fully mastering a specific subject. That's also where my strength lies—I've got a solid grasp of many topics and an insatiable appetite to learn more. This inherent curiosity keeps me driven to expand my skill set and venture into uncharted territories. Here's to embracing the joy of learning across the board!

From Passion to Pint

Date:

Title: From Passion to Pint: Brewer Matt Tweedy’s Journey to Mastery

Job Titles:Camera Operator, Motion Graphic Designer, Editor, Lighting/Gaffer

Software/hardware: After Effects, Premier Pro, Audition, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Nikon D160 with kit 50mm lens, led light panels, reflectors, tripod, shotgun mic, Zoom Recorder

Summary

The third semester I worked on a group project that was documentary style video. We chose to do it on a brewpub and its owner in the Ottawa area called Tooth and Nail. The story is of Matt Tweedy and his discovery of wanting to start the business and some insight into the brewing process he is passionate about.

We had six weeks to complete the project among our other classes and their projects. We made it to all weekly meetings with the professor and had all the documentation ready for presentation. At times we were even ahead of schedule. One example is I had some samples of the type of motion graphics and infographic ready to show the professor.

He was so encouraged by our work in progress that he slotted us for the first to present to the two Video Production classes combined presentation that one of the program coordinators came to watch some of them. I am very proud of what we produced together.

Filming Day

My roles in the production of the video span the whole process from the brainstorming to the final edit. The on-set roles I performed were setting up and monitoring the main camera used in the interview and to help set up the lighting. I also helped capture some of the first set of b-roll.

Two highlights I have from this part are nailing the creative treatment with the shallow depth of field and catching the LED panel reflecting in the glasses of our stand-in.

The creative treatment we were aiming for was a soft background while still having it present in the shot. We also wanted it to have a warm and casual conversational feel with the subjects being interviewed.

The subject of our interview also wears glasses. The way I worked around this is placing a reflector at the foot of the light and turning the panel to aim directly at it. This aided the key light which we had set at a higher angle by filling the face from the bottom and front, softening up the shadows. Making sure these were taken care of in-camera made post production that much easier.

Motion Graphics

My major role was creating the motion graphics and animations which wasn’t initially slated for me to do. I created our group’s logo and made the animation for it. The original design was made using vector illustrations that translated into After Effects easily. I kept the animation simple and to the point but feel like it is impactful.

One of the first motion graphics I had in mind when brainstorming was to take the existing logos the brewery had already on their cans. The art style used was a vintage, hand-drawn look to them. To highlight the style, I created what looked like paper cutouts of the different layers I felt were there.

I used Illustrator to do this, since at this point, I didn’t know that you could import Photoshop documents and the layers would be treated the same way. Outside of the little bit we had done in class, this was the first animation that I made in After Effects. I created a few of these, one of which I also took initiative to learn Duik Angela to make. One of the animations was used by a group member to create the lower thirds.

I followed some tutorials to create the map animation and for the inspiration of the timeline infographic.I cut the audio from the interview to make it concise and edited it. I synced the animation timing to the key moments in the audio clip. The images echo that of the branding already established by the brand. To further bring in the branding, I used a bright red for the pins and the markers on the map, as well as the bold red line of the timeline that looks like the ones on the top of the can.

Finally, I did the credits. I modified a template to feel like the bubbles and foam of a golden beer. As a team we didn’t see obvious places that my logo animations could work but wanted to make sure they were used. At the time of the decision, I only had three created and needed a total of five to match the number of us that were on the team. Since we each played a part in so many roles, we opted to not have titles. I felt that it was important to include thanks to the establishment, the creator of the logos, the school, and the Algonquin peoples on top of those we had interviewed. Unfortunately, our main subject’s father had passed away during the postproduction of this piece. We included a tribute to him before the credits roll since he was an important figure in our subject’s life.

Editing

I had the role of final editing. The main tasks I had were color grading the second camera that we used and to keyframe all of the cuts made in the audio. Due to the differences in cameras, there was a dramatic difference in the white balance between the two. Since the video was already cut and sequenced, I used an adjustment layer to quickly apply the same settings over all of the clips. I then went back and tweaked the ones that were a little further off. Unfortunately, the room tone we initially took was no longer with the project. The main way I got around this was by finding a pause between a question being asked and the answer that followed. It wasn’t the ideal situation, but at least there was room tone under all of the cuts and the keyframes done. Finally, I added the music. I identified the key moments and made sure to place the more emotional break in the music on those parts and blended the ends of the music with the next. We limited our video to have two tracks, one was used for the interview and the second was used for the animations and the credits. We wanted to make sure there was a energizing rock tune but still cinematic to carry along the narrative,

Challenges

There were some challenges with this project that made for good learning experiences. When working on a large project, I like to work as if the timeline is about a quarter less than what is given. I’ve experienced unexpected setbacks or interruptions, and by working with the decrease in allotted time allowed me to stay on track when that happens. If there is no disruption, it allows for more polishing time. Unfortunately, when working in a group not everyone shares this method, and it can be quite stressful. One way I managed this was my self-guided learning on After Effects even though I didn’t have the footage at the time. I built a template that the timing could be modified based on the audio we got. I also worked on other projects that had a similar deadline. The other challenge was limiting the working hours on this project. It became a passion project as well as wanting to make something that would pass the assignment. The number of hours spent on this is outside the realm of what the original scope of the promo video offered to the subject in the video.

Overall, this project was a great learning experience and has led me to learning a lot more about After Effects and motion graphics.

Passages

Date: Dec 8th. 2024

Title:Passages

Job Titles:Motion Graphic Designer, Editor, Writer

Software/hardware: After Effects

Summary

During Symbols, Texts and Their Meanings course there were many topics and I was assigned a visual demonstration of my understanding. I was enjoying learning After Effects and wanted to practice further and apply it to this assignment. It features keying overlays, creating a parallax effect on a static image, animating the blink effect and video editing.

Target Audience

Young Adults to Seniors with a Western/North American Cultural Perspective.

Young adults to Seniors that have a western/north american type of understanding of the symbols and icons of the life stages. I didn’t include any people in traditional dress of other countries/traditions. A specific example is the right of passage of learning to drive in the mid teenage years. Many other cultures, the education stops at primary school due to many reasons like working in the family business or the inability to pay for it. The same reason that college isn’t a natural stepping stone in growing up.

Words/Symbols/signs

Empty bed in juxtaposition to the series of elderly photographs makes the connection that they have passed away without having to say so. The phrase “in loving memory” is also a symbol for the death of a loved one. The phrase invokes that feeling of loss. Another index/symbol that I used is the fact that each stage of life the images are one quarter of a second shorter after each blink transition. It represents the feeling that as you grow older the passing of time seems to go faster and faster. Then when the concept of loss happens, the clips are then extended to the length that the infant clips are set to. It gives the audience a chance to reflect and feel loss and give them a chance to process what they saw and connect to the concept of time as well as loss.

Fonts

The main font that I picked is Palatino Linotype and I chose it because it is clean, easy to read with the limited time for the blink transition. It also gives me the feeling of classic and timelessness.I also used Poked because it is similar to a kid's handwriting. It is used to remind the viewer of writing in school and that feeling of a crayon in their hand. Poked evolves into Indie Flower. A more mature version of handwriting. Then Source Code Pro is used to symbolize the way technology/computers become a centralized factor of this stage of life. The feeling that Freestyle Script gives helps bring emphasis by imitating the handwriting that a relative would use on a paper check.

Brochure

Date: Dec 9, 2022 & April 21, 2023

Title: Haunting Beautiful Cosmetics Brochure and Products

Job Titles: Illustrator, Copywriter, Graphic Designer

Software/hardware: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop

Summary:

This brand and line of products are the result of two different assignments. The focus on one assignment was precise and concise writing featuring products or services that didn't exist. I used my skills in Photoshop and Illustrator to stylize the brochure for a novel cosmetic company. I used this project as inspiration for my Shopify assignment that I had in Content Management Systems. I made two additional products, a lip gloss and a toothpaste. I wanted to have an elegant but a dark look. I took inspiration for the logo and color pallet from old wine, perfume and potion labels. The content is inspired by historical horror movies. Filled with puns, the text adds a fun and humorous side without taking away from the sleek look of the document.

Logo and Design

The project started with the idea of moonscreen, which would be applied like sunscreen. The concepts that helped shape the logo design are the words “luxury” and “premium”. I used rich colors like red, black and gold to sell the idea that this was a product that those of high class would use. Black is a color that is often associated with night and I used it as the foundation to apply the other elements. The deep red compliments the darkness that comes from the black. The gold adds a contrasting highlight as well as to communicate the idea of it being that luxury item. The shape with the die cut corners was directly inspired by the labels. It also gives the logo a bold shape that is neither feminine or masculine. The bats were initially hand drawn and were created to push the narrative of the “creature of the night” and add an element of elegance with the fine detail. The drawings were then translated into vectors to make sure that all of the symmetry lined up.

Copy

To create the copy for the brochure, I drew inspiration from a cast of classic horror story and film characters, infusing the text with a blend of puns and satire. The catalyst for this creative process was the enigmatic aristocrat Dorian Gray, as imagined by both Oscar Wilde and Alan Moore. Mr. Gray's review, 'I can't see myself without it,' sets the tone for the brochure's whimsical yet eerie atmosphere. In both Wilde's 'The Picture of Dorian Gray' and Moore's portrayal, Dorian hides away his portrait, a private reflection of his moral degradation, concealed from prying eyes within his home.

Then there are the doctor recommendations. Dr. Victor Frankenstein is my favorite of the recommendations. He goes to say “Each ingredient has been patiently and expertly collected and combined in a way that brings new life to the beauty and skin care industry.” This highlights the base of the story of Frankenstein by Mary Shelly in which Dr. Frankenstein creates a new life from collected body parts. I had a lot of fun trying to find all of the ways I could play with words.

Products

The initial product is the moonscreen called Moon Scream and the two products that were created for my Shopify assignment are a line of lip glosses based on poisonous flowers, Good Night Kiss, and fang paste, Pearly Bites. I continued to use the same base logo shape as the Moon Scream but on the corners the images were changed to relate to the new products. The colors that were used are black and gold with the main color related to the variation on the product.

Bonus

It hasn’t been put up for sale yet, but I did make a repeating pattern to use for fabrics and wallpaper using the bats. I used Adobe Illustrator to create a seamless pattern in two different size variations. I intend on getting some fabric printed to make a dress out of and I know someone who wants to order it as wallpaper for her dining room.

Photoshop

I love to explore photoshop so I expanded on the assignment here. The parameters was to cut out two subjects and place them on a different background. The movie Rocky Horror Picture Show was my inspiration. I found a font that is very similar to the movie title and captured images of two of the characters. I used colors present in the film as well.

I used many tools to create the background and the golden seal. All of the text are modified quotes or a play-on-words from the film.
