About Me

I'm a student at Algonquin College of Applied Arts and Technology, on track to graduate in April 2024. My college ride has been quite the adventure, exploring a mix of subjects—from web design and photography to videography and UX design. But you know what really caught my eye? Motion graphics! That's also where my strength lies—I've got a solid grasp of many topics and an insatiable appetite to learn more. This inherent curiosity keeps me driven to expand my skill set and venture into uncharted territories. Here's to embracing the joy of learning across the board!

From Passion to Pint

Date: Dec 10, 2023

Title: From Passion to Pint: Brewer Matt Tweedy’s Journey to Mastery

Job Titles:Camera Operator, Motion Graphic Designer, Editor, Lighting/Gaffer

Software/hardware: After Effects, Premier Pro, Audition, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Nikon D160 with kit 50mm lens, LED light panels, reflectors, tripod, shotgun mic, Zoom Recorder

Summary

The third semester I worked on a group project that was documentary style video. We chose to do it on a brewpub and its owner in the Ottawa area called Tooth and Nail. The story is of Matt Tweedy and his discovery of wanting to start the business and some insight into the brewing process he is passionate about.

We had six weeks to complete the project among our other classes and their projects. We made it to all weekly meetings with the professor and had all the documentation ready for presentation. At times we were even ahead of schedule. Such as when is I had some samples of the type of motion graphics and infographic ready to show the professor.

He was so excited by our work in progress that he slotted us for the first to present. He combined the two Video Production classes to present together. One of the program coordinators came to watch the first four. I am very proud of what we produced together.

My roles in the production of the video span the whole process from the brainstorming to the final edit. The on-set roles I performed were setting up and monitoring the main camera used in the interview and to help set up the lighting. I also helped capture some of the first set of b-roll.

Two highlights I have from this part are nailing the creative treatment with the shallow depth of field and catching the LED panel reflecting in the glasses of our stand-in.

The creative treatment we were aiming for was a soft background while still having it present in the shot. We also wanted it to have a warm and casual conversational feel with the subjects being interviewed.

The subject of our interview also wears glasses. The way I worked around this is placing a reflector at the foot of the light and turning the panel to aim directly at it. This aided the key light which we had set at a higher angle by filling the face from the bottom and front, softening up the shadows. Making sure these were taken care of in-camera made post production that much easier.

My major role was creating the motion graphics and animations which wasn’t initially slated for me to do. I created our group’s logo and made the animation for it. The original design was made using vector illustrations that translated into After Effects easily. I kept the animation simple and to the point but still impactful.

One of the first motion graphics I had in mind when brainstorming was to take the existing logos the brewery had already on their cans. The art used was vintage and had a hand-drawn look. To highlight the style, I created what looked like paper cutouts of the different layers I felt were there.

I used Illustrator to do this, since at this point, I didn’t know that you could import Photoshop documents and the layers would be treated the same way. Outside of the little bit we had done in class, this was the first animation that I made in After Effects. I created a few of these, one of which I also took initiative to learn Duik Angela to make. One of the animations was used by a group member to create the lower thirds.

I followed some tutorials to create the map animation and for the inspiration of the timeline infographic.I cut the audio from the interview to make it concise and edited it. I synced the animation timing to the key moments in the audio clip. The images echo that of the branding already established by the owner. To further bring in the styling, I used a bright red for the pins and the markers on the map, as well as the bold red line of the timeline that looks like the ones on the top of the can.

Finally, I did the credits. I modified a template to feel like the bubbles and foam of a golden beer. As a team we didn’t see obvious places that my logo animations could work but wanted to make sure they were used. At the time of the decision, I only had three created and needed a total of five to match the number of us that were on the team. Since we each played a part in so many roles, we opted to not have titles. I felt that it was important to include thanks to the establishment, the creator of the logos, the school, and the Algonquin peoples on top of those we had interviewed. Unfortunately, our main subject’s father had passed away during the post production of this piece. We included a tribute to him before the credits roll since he was an important figure in our subject’s life.

I had the role of final editing. The main tasks I had were color grading the second camera that we used and to keyframe all of the cuts made in the audio. Due to the differences in cameras, there was a dramatic difference in the white balance between the two. Since the video was already cut and sequenced, I used an adjustment layer to quickly apply the same settings over all of the clips. I then went back and tweaked the ones that were a little further off. Unfortunately, the room tone we initially took was no longer with the project. The main way I got around this was by finding a pause between a question being asked and the answer that followed. It wasn’t the ideal situation, but at least there was room tone under all of the cuts and the keyframes done. Finally, I added the music. I identified the key moments and made sure to place the more emotional break in the music on those parts and blended the ends of the music with the next. We limited our video to have two tracks, one was used for the interview and the second was used for the animations and the credits. We wanted to make sure there was a energizing rock tune but still cinematic to carry along the narrative,

There were some challenges with this project that made for good learning experiences. When working on a large project, I like to work as if the timeline is about a quarter less than what is given. I’ve experienced unexpected setbacks or interruptions, and by working with the decrease in allotted time allowed me to stay on track when that happens. If there is no disruption, it allows for more polishing time. Unfortunately, when working in a group not everyone shares this method, and it can be quite stressful. One way I managed this was my self-guided learning on After Effects even though I didn’t have the footage at the time. I built a template that the timing could be modified based on the audio we got. I also worked on other projects that had a similar deadline. The other challenge was limiting the working hours on this project. It became a passion project as well as wanting to make something that would pass the assignment.

Overall, this project was a great learning experience and has led me to learning a lot more about After Effects and motion graphics.

AllerCheck Connect

Date: April 15, 2024

Title:AllerCheck Connect

Job Titles:Motion Graphic Designer, Editor, Writer, Graphic Design, Illustrator

Software/hardware: After Effects, Adobe Illustrator

Summary

A group assignment in level four that a next gen technology was to be conceptualized that required two devices to talk to each other and feature a distinct UI interaction and make a promo video for it. I was grouped up with peers that I had been looking forward to working with. We had three weekly meetings with the professor to cover the topics of the pitch, the storyboards and then a rough edit of the final product. We also took advantage of an optional fourth meeting to get feedback on the final draft and improvements before the turn-in.

Initial Designs

We brainstormed a lot of different ideas, trying to keep focus on the problem and not the solution. It was a challenge since it is a new skill and is often taught to think of ways to fix an issue in childhood without exploring in depth what the real problem could actually be. A portable allergy testing device is what was agreed on. There were two ideas that were sketched out. Sadly, the parts for the mock product that was visually appealing were not available in time. It was the one due to be constructed out of a pipette controller and a pipette or straw. A third design needed to be pivoted to, one of which the base could be delivered on time. We went with a foldable meat thermometer. It had a similar method of use to the pipette and would be easily adapted into our vision.

UX Storyboard

I was in charge of the UX storyboards. This project didn’t require the storyboards related to specific shots but to focus on the interactions between the user and the UI. I kept to the story we also wanted to tell in the video and what was used as the treatment. I used some stock images to draw over in order to speed up the process.

After Effects

I took on the tasks of the scenes with the device and creating the infographics. To attempt to make the tracking on the device easier and because at the time of filming the logo wasn’t finalized, I created a blue and green screen sticker with tracking dots to put on our mockup device. I created the shapes in Adobe Illustrator and exported it as an SVG format to use in Cricut Design Space. Unfortunately I was out of ink, so my initial plan of creating a single sticker was out. I was able to still create what I had envisioned by using green and blue vinyl sticker paper. To minimize interference through reflection while tracking, I used a clear matte nail polish to take away the shine. In the end I didn’t utilize the blue and green for keying but the color contrast and the tracking dots were pivotal in being able to track and fine tune in Mocha AE.

The tracking was the most difficult part of this project. Though steps to track easier were taken, the excitement while filming overshadowed the attention to how difficult the shots would be to actually track. A few of the shots the movement was very quick or there was occlusion and the tracking tools within After Effects struggled with them. Between determination and not having the ability to reshoot, I persevered. I watched tutorials around the use of Mocha AE and focused on Adjust Track and Manual Track. Through that I also learned how to stabilize shots without using Warp Stabilizer. I applied it to some shots to help with some of the jitter but keep the hand held look that we were going for. Other than learning more about tracking with Mocha, I also learned some valuable knowledge on what to look out for while on set if tracking is going to be needed.

The information that I picked for the infographics I wanted to be relatable and to show the impact that food allergies have on those around us through the number of people affected. To create the animations, I looked up some tutorials and modified the process to suit the information that was being displayed. One of the tricker ones was the list of allergens tested. The timing of the radial wipe start and end to the same points on the trim paths with the offset took a little fiddling around. I am happy with how it turned out.

Moving Forward

I am overall satisfied with this project. There are a few things I would do differently moving forward. The changes mainly revolve around shooting day. Although the camera work was well done, I think using a tripod more or renting something like a Gimbal would increase the quality. In post, the framing was adjusted to address a few of the shots that had the young actor looking up at the cameraman for cues. Lastly, not reviewing and directing the shots in a way that kept the difficulty of tracking in mind, as that was a major part in out assignment.

Timeless Drift

Date: April 10, 2023

Title: Timeless Drift

Job Titles: Director, Editor, Camera Operator

Software/hardware: Excel, Premiere Pro, Nikon D160, 50mm kit lense

Summary

This was part one of a two part group final for Video Foundations. We were in pairs and tasked with a creative video and an instructional one that were one and a half minutes to three minutes long. We went through the entire process from a pitch meeting, pre production, production and finally post production. My partner and I came up with the idea of a John Hughs style montage that gives the feeling of getting ready in the 80’s paired with a sharp contrast of being in the 2020’s.

Pre-Production

After we came up with our idea we started drafting up the documents needed to pitch to our professor. I created the production schedule, equipment list, the budget sheet as if we had to rent equipment from a shop, and the shot list. My partner made the storyboards and soundscape. With our pitch approved, I went to the thrift store to try and find some items to sell the 80’s. I was able to score an intercom, the alarm clock that everyone had and a sweater that oozed the aesthetic we were going for. With our plan in place we moved onto production.

Production

We started out filming the ending sequence first. The reason is my partner was the actor, and we wanted him to look older and like the rat-race has a weight on him. For that he grew out his facial hair as much as time would allow and would later shave to play the role on the rest of the film. This added some pressure to make sure the shots worked and to get enough footage initially because the schedule wouldn’t allow time for the growth to happen followed by a re-shoot..

Post Production

I was in charge of editing the footage while my partner was creating the foley and using music he created. This was the first time I had the lead in the editing. I had only done the rough cuts before. One scene I am particularly proud of is when the door is shut to give the impression of getting changed. That transition was envisioned the day of the shoot. The camera had gotten moved between shots. I used the rulers in Premiere Pro to line up the edges of the window, color grading and using a dissolve fade between cuts to make it look like it was one shot.

Challenges

Another challenge was not using licensed music, we really wanted to use “Don’t You (Forget About Me)” by Simple Minds and The Police. To meet the requirements of the assignment we used copyright free music, however if I were to make a final version of this piece it would include that licensed track as I feel it sells the nostalgic setting that I had envisioned.

Lastly, as the character was scanning through the radio it was planned to have “doom and gloom” clips of broadcasters saying things along the lines of “housing prices continue to rise” and “the pandemic doesn’t have an end in sight”. However this did not make it into the final deliverables.

In combination with the licensed music, I feel that the radio clips would have served to better represent the contrast of the carefree nostalgic mood with the unwelcome introduction of bleakness and modern reality. I believe these final touches with minor edit changes would have made the video much stronger. I wish that I had advocated for their inclusion more but at least moving forward I recognize the value of not compromising on my creative vision and I intend to revisit this project to bring it up to the level I can be creatively satisfied with.

Brochure

Date: Dec 9, 2022 & April 21, 2023

Title: Haunting Beautiful Cosmetics Brochure and Products

Job Titles: Illustrator, Copywriter, Graphic Designer

Software/hardware: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop

Summary:

This brand and line of products are the result of two different assignments. The focus on one assignment was precise and concise writing featuring products or services that didn't exist. I used my skills in Photoshop and Illustrator to stylize the brochure for a novel cosmetic company. I used this project as inspiration for my Shopify assignment that I had in Content Management Systems. I made two additional products, a lip gloss and a toothpaste. I wanted to have an elegant but a dark look. I took inspiration for the logo and color pallet from old wine, perfume and potion labels. The content is inspired by historical horror movies. Filled with puns, the text adds a fun and humorous side without taking away from the sleek look of the document.

Logo and Design

The project started with the idea of moonscreen, which would be applied like sunscreen. The concepts that helped shape the logo design are the words “luxury” and “premium”. I used rich colors like red, black and gold to sell the idea that this was a product that those of high class would use. Black is a color that is often associated with night and I used it as the foundation to apply the other elements. The deep red compliments the darkness that comes from the black. The gold adds a contrasting highlight as well as to communicate the idea of it being that luxury item. The shape with the die cut corners was directly inspired by the labels. It also gives the logo a bold shape that is neither feminine or masculine. The bats were initially hand drawn and were created to push the narrative of the “creature of the night” and add an element of elegance with the fine detail. The drawings were then translated into vectors to make sure that all of the symmetry lined up.

Copy

To create the copy for the brochure, I drew inspiration from a cast of classic horror story and film characters, infusing the text with a blend of puns and satire. The catalyst for this creative process was the enigmatic aristocrat Dorian Gray, as imagined by both Oscar Wilde and Alan Moore. Mr. Gray's review, 'I can't see myself without it,' sets the tone for the brochure's whimsical yet eerie atmosphere. In both Wilde's 'The Picture of Dorian Gray' and Moore's portrayal, Dorian hides away his portrait, a private reflection of his moral degradation, concealed from prying eyes within his home.

Then there are the doctor recommendations. Dr. Victor Frankenstein is my favorite of the recommendations. He goes to say “Each ingredient has been patiently and expertly collected and combined in a way that brings new life to the beauty and skin care industry.” This highlights the base of the story of Frankenstein by Mary Shelly in which Dr. Frankenstein creates a new life from collected body parts. I had a lot of fun trying to find all of the ways I could play with words.

Products

The initial product is the moonscreen called Moon Scream and the two products that were created for my Shopify assignment are a line of lip glosses based on poisonous flowers, Good Night Kiss, and fang paste, Pearly Bites. I continued to use the same base logo shape as the Moon Scream but on the corners the images were changed to relate to the new products. The colors that were used are black and gold with the main color related to the variation on the product.

Bonus

It hasn’t been put up for sale yet, but I did make a repeating pattern to use for fabrics and wallpaper using the bats. I used Adobe Illustrator to create a seamless pattern in two different size variations. I intend on getting some fabric printed to make a dress out of and I know someone who wants to order it as wallpaper for her dining room.

Book Cover

Date: Nov 15, 2022

Title: Become A Man In Just 7 Days Book Cover

Job Titles: Compositor, Designer

Software/hardware: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop

Summary

This started out as an exercise for Digital Graphics. The prompt was to simply take one image, cut out a figure and place it on another background. Through experience and other tutorials I knew that the way that was being shown wasn’t the ideal way to achieve the same effect but allow much more flexibility.

The Start

The method that was taught is using the quick select to select the subject, copy then paste the selection on another document. I used the Place tool to add the full image of Rocky and an image of tiles into the document. I used the quick select tool to get most of the selection done around Rocky, then I used the “Select and Mask” function to refine the selection with focus on the areas with a lot of texture/edges like the hair. I then used it to mask the image instead of removing the background as it was shown in class. By using the mask I am able to preserve the original image in-case I need to make any adjustments to the selection. I found it best to work as non-destructively as possible when it comes to compositing images.

Inspiration

Once I added some adjustment layers to the background to make the tile look pink to mimic the setting in which the scene took place, I became inspired to attempt to make something similar to a DVD cover. I attempted to remake a DVD cover with this same film in my high school Graphic Design course. I love satire and puns. I found this to be a perfect place to play with them. The title, the synopsis and the inspiration for the seal of the book all come from the song “I can Make You A Man”. There are quotes from the characters and other details used in the book to bring it all together.

How

I couldn’t find an image of Dr. Frank ‘n’ Furter that wasn’t over used or I felt like would work as the author portrait. I used a combination of Snipping Tool and a digital version of the movie I own to go frame by frame to capture the image I did. It is the scene in which Dr. Frank ‘n’ Furter is introducing his latest experiment, the creation of Rocky. Similar to the way I removed the background from Rocky, I did the same thing to Dr. Frank ‘n’ Furter, paying extra attention to his hair due to the complex texture of his curls and the fact the original image had a light background that would be very noticeable against a darker color.

In the lyrics of the song there is a line that states “The Charles Atlas Seal of Approval” and that sparked the inspiration for the gold seal on the book. I created the seal from scratch using a combination of masks, effects, adjustment layers - the works.

I found a knack for making product mock ups, and I applied that to this piece. It was originally just a flat image. It is amazing how this comes to life when it looks like an object that can be held and exists in the real world.

Title: Sassy Cat Studios Branding

Job Titles: Illustrator, Copywriter, Graphic Designer

Software/hardware: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop

Summary

This is the branding that I developed initially in high school, and then through level one and two of the IMD program. If I were to open my own studio this is the branding I would want it under. The faceless cat is something that has stayed with me for nearly 20 years, so if it has lasted that long for me I think that says something. I would like to blend the old with the new.

The Original

This is the original design that I came up with used on a stationary set. The font choice was a very uneducated choice and they don’t match. I do still love the way I used the cat on the letterhead. Outside of that I hadn’t applied the logo in any other way.

The Updated

I turned my original logo into a brand through a few of my classes in my IMD program. In Digital Graphics, I redesigned my logo and came up with a mini branding guide. In User Experience I, I continued to brand myself through personas, mood boards, and wireframes. In Web Dev II we applied that branding to a web page that was developed as our final. For now I am going to develop and refine this branding until I am going to do more freelance work.

My favorite part about this branding is the icon that I made using the initials and the three stars of the logo. I also utilized that icon into a repeating background motif. I also like how there is the space where the word "Studios" fits and if the studio gets large enough for multiple departments, the name of the department can fit there. That is featured on page two of the branding guide.

Astronomy Picture of the Day

Date: Dec 10, 2023

Title: APOD API Assignment

Job Titles: Illustrator, Graphic Designer, Web Design, Web Developer

Software/hardware: Adobe Photoshop, Visual Studio Code, Git

Summary

This is the final project for Web Development III. The focus of the assignment was JavaScript and the use of API calls. I chose to do the NASA Astronomy Picture of the Day (APOD) one. When I was looking around at libraries I found one called TurnJS that inspired pretty much the whole site. TurnJS turns the website content into an interactive book where the pages bend and move like paper. One of the requirements was to have favorites stored in local storage, and I thought it would be neat to have it as a photo album. For the final turn in, I had to pass up on that idea to accomplish the task at hand.

Assets

One of the fonts I chose is Exo as the main font. It is clean and easy to read, but gives a sci-fi feel to it. Consolas is the "header" font that has the same feel as Exo but is a little bit blockier.

I made the books and telescope grow on hover for the desktop, and added a bit of a drop shadow that acts like a glow. I have intentions on creating a animation for the window at a minimum. All the assets minus the pictures from the APOD site are going to have a cartoon/hand drawn effect. It will give a hand crafted, cute/whimsical feel to the site overall.

JavaScript

JavaScript is something that doesn't come to me naturally. When prompted to write something from scratch, I have a tendency to freeze and have difficulty starting. If I am given a foundation of what it needs to be, I can typically modify it to do what I need it to do if it is a simple function. On this project, there were some major hurdles for me to figure out. The biggest one was getting the book to dynamically build based on the number of images in local storage. I sought outside help since the code wasn’t reacting the way that I had expected it too.

Between their knowledge of other programming languages and my understanding of HTML/CSS we did get it working, but at a great time expense and after the due date of the project. For the turn-in I opted to turn in one that has a gallery based on a previous assignment for two major factors; it wasn’t working fully and to the parameters of the assignment as well as the fact that it wasn’t entirely my work. When I continue working on it to get it to the point that I want, the book asset will be implemented and credit will be given to the person that helped me.

Changes Moving Forward

Other than implementing the book as the favorites, I have a few other changes I would like to make to finish this as a portfolio piece. First off, I would like to divide the site into multiple pages again. When I first developed the project it was four pages, but due to the assignment parameters, it had to have one HTML, one CSS and one JS file. I am quite proud of condensing my JS file and getting that one to work with no errors.

Another thing that I would like to change is the book buttons on the home page. I will keep the favorites as a book, but the past pictures are going to change into a tablet, since that makes more sense with how the API call works as well as how I designed the page for it. I also want to create some animations, likely through Lotties, to bring the whole thing to life and make it more like an application than a web site based on my knowledge at this point. Lastly, I want to clean my code up, validate my HTML/CSS and add the accessibility that was taught in Web Development II.
